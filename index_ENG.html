Definitions and Signaling Logic

🚦 Automatic Block on Double Track (BAD): Railway signaling system where each track is used exclusively in one direction. For example, from "A" to "B" in one direction and from "D" to "E" in the other. Only track locking/unlocking is allowed, with no possibility of bi-directional use.

🛤️ Track Circuit: Each section into which the railway track is divided. Each section has a signal (traffic light) indicating whether it is free or occupied. It detects train presence and ensures safety between trains.

🔀 Switch (or turnout): Mechanism allowing trains to change from one track to another. It can be in a straight position (continues on the same track) or diverging (toward another track). Its configuration affects the signal the driver sees.

The driver sees the signal of the next track circuit they will enter, not the one the train is currently on.
